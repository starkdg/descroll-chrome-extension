Most of us open a new tab dozens of times a day. Usually, this leads
to a "distraction loop"—an unplanned trip to a social media site where
an algorithm decides what you should see.

DeScroll changes the narrative. It replaces the empty space of your
"New Tab" page with a high-quality, infinite feed sourced entirely
from the websites you actually care about.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------

Why DeScroll?

We all have a habit of scrolling. Instead of fighting that habit,
DeScroll makes it useful. By pulling content from your own
bookmarks, it ensures that every time you pause between tasks,
you’re greeted with relevant, interesting articles rather than "junk
food" content from a social media timeline.

Key Features

   * Turn Bookmarks into a Feed: Simply put your favorite blogs, news
     sites, or portfolios into a folder (defaulted to "MyFeed"), and
     DeScroll transforms them into a modern, scrolling interface.
   * Automatic Content Discovery: You don’t need to know what an RSS
     feed is. Just bookmark a homepage, and DeScroll’s engine will
     automatically find the latest articles, blog posts, or updates
     for you.
   * Fresh Every Time: DeScroll remembers what you’ve already looked
     at. It uses a rolling "seen" window to filter out old links,
     ensuring that every time you open a tab, there is something new
     to discover.
   * Intentional Variety: You can configure how many different sources
     you want to see at once. Whether you want a deep dive into one
     site or a broad mix from ten different domains, you are in total
     control of the algorithm.
   * Privacy-First & Lightweight: DeScroll runs entirely in your
     browser. It doesn't track your data, doesn't require an account,
     and is designed to be lightning-fast so it never slows down your
     browsing.

How to Get Started

   1. Install the extension.
   2. Create a bookmark folder named "MyFeed" (or customize the name
      in the extension settings).
   3. Add your favorite sites to that folder.
   4. Open a new tab and start scrolling through content that actually
      matters to you.

Stop scrolling for the algorithm. Start scrolling for yourself.
